Norse mythology, Jörmungandr (Old Norse: Jǫrmungandr, pronounced [ˈjɔrmoŋˌɡɑndz̠], meaning "huge monster"), also known as the Midgard Serpent or World Serpent (Old Norse: Miðgarðsormr [ˈmiðˌɡɑrðsˌormz̠]), is a sea serpent and the middle child of Loki and the giantess Angrboða.

According to the Prose Edda, Odin took Loki's three children by Angrboða—the wolf Fenrir, Hel, and Jörmungandr—and tossed Jörmungandr into the great ocean that encircles Midgard.
